### 14. Fermented Kimchi
**Difficulty:** Hard (Fermentation) | **Prep:** 20 min | **Fermentation:** 2-5 days

**Nutritional Info (Per Serving - 1g):**
- Calories: 23-38 kcal
- Protein: 1-2g
- Carbohydrates: 4-8g
- Fiber: 2-3g
- Contains: Live probiotics, antioxidants
- **Best For:** Gut health, probiotic source, fermented foods

**Health Benefits:**
- Rich in probiotics (live beneficial bacteria)
- Supports digestive health
- Contains vitamin C (increases during fermentation)
- Anti-inflammatory properties
- Boosts immunity
- Aids nutrient absorption
- Contains B vitamins (especially B12)

**Ingredients:**
- 1 head napa cabbage
- 2 tbsp salt (for brining)
- 2 tbsp gochugaru (Korean chili flakes) or Indian red chili
- 4 cloves garlic (minced)
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger (grated)
- 3 tbsp fish sauce or soy sauce
- 1 tbsp sugar
- 4 green onions (chopped)
- 1 tbsp sesame seeds

**Method:**
1. Cut cabbage into bite-sized pieces
2. Dissolve salt in water and soak cabbage for 1-2 hours
3. Rinse cabbage thoroughly and drain well
4. Mix gochugaru, garlic, ginger, fish sauce, sugar, sesame seeds
5. Combine with cabbage and green onions
6. Pack tightly into glass jar, pressing down firmly
7. Leave 1-2 inches headspace
8. Cover with cloth and let sit at room temperature
9. In summer: 1-2 days fermentation
10. In winter: 4-5 days fermentation
11. Taste daily and refrigerate when preferred level of sourness reached
12. Lasts several weeks in refrigerator
